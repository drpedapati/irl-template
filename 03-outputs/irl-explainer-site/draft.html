2. Your Expertise, Not the AI's new Toy 8

This isn't about AI features or code. It's about your reasoning, your judgment, and your critical review of the output.

Address the elephant in the room: "But AI makes mistakes." Yes — so do human assistants. The difference isn't whether mistakes happen, it's whether you have a system for catching them. IRL puts you in the driver's seat. You write out your reasoning in the plan. The AI produces a draft. You review the output critically — not the AI's process, but the actual files it produced. If something is wrong, you loop back: ask for clarification, run a verification, restate your reasoning more precisely, and iterate until the output earns your trust.

This is no different from managing a capable human assistant. You wouldn't hand someone a vague instruction and blindly accept the result. You'd review, redirect, refine. IRL just gives that loop a stable structure so nothing falls through the cracks.

Key emphasis: this is about your domain expertise, not AI features. The AI doesn't need to be perfect. It needs to be directable. And you need a system for reviewing what it produces.
