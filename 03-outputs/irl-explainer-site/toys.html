Idempotent vs. Non-Idempotent

Press "Run" on each side. Watch the dots settle into place. One side always produces the same pattern. The other never does.

Idempotent
Same inputs, same outputs. Press Run.
Non-Idempotent
Same inputs, different outputs. Press Run.

The left side is safe to rerun: dots always land in the same places, like following a recipe exactly. The right side drifts: new dots appear, positions shift, and you can't tell which run was "correct." Idempotency is what makes iteration trustworthy.
